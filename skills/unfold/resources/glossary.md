# 📖 Unfold — What Do These Scales Mean?

*Let yourself unfold.*

These are impressions inferred from your journaling sessions — not tests you took or
clinical measurements. They borrow the structure of established screening scales to
help notice patterns, but they aren't validated results and they aren't a diagnosis.
The direction things move over time matters far more than any single reading.

---

## PHQ-9
**Tracks:** Depression (low mood, energy, interest, sleep, self-worth)

The most widely used depression screener. Catches patterns you might not notice
day-to-day — things like creeping loss of interest, changes in sleep, or a quiet
sense of worthlessness that builds over weeks.

## GAD-7
**Tracks:** Anxiety (worry, restlessness, tension, irritability)

Tracks whether anxious feelings are occasional or becoming a pattern. Useful for
noticing when background worry starts interfering with your ability to relax or focus.

## BHS
**Tracks:** Hopelessness (pessimism about the future, loss of motivation)

One of the strongest predictors of suicidal thinking — important to track even when
scores are low. Hopelessness isn't just sadness; it's the belief that things won't
get better.

## PSS-10
**Tracks:** Perceived stress (feeling overwhelmed, out of control)

Not about what's happening to you, but how stressed you *feel* — which is what
actually affects your health. Two people in the same situation can have very
different stress responses.

## PCL-5
**Tracks:** PTSD symptoms (flashbacks, avoidance, hypervigilance)

Only scored when trauma-related content comes up in a session. Covers intrusive
memories, avoidance behaviors, negative mood shifts, and heightened arousal.

## PHQ-15
**Tracks:** Physical symptoms linked to stress (headaches, fatigue, pain)

Your body often registers stress before your mind does. This scale tracks somatic
symptoms that may have psychological roots — not to dismiss them, but to connect
the dots.

## ISI
**Tracks:** Sleep problems (falling asleep, staying asleep, impact on functioning)

Sleep is both a symptom and a cause of most mental health difficulties. Even brief
mentions of sleep trouble are worth tracking because sleep affects everything else.

## DERS-18
**Tracks:** Emotion regulation (ability to manage difficult feelings effectively)

Tracks the *skills* side — not whether you feel bad, but whether you can work with
difficult emotions when they show up. Are you able to stay on course when upset, or
do things tend to spiral?

## AUDIT-C / DAST-10
**Tracks:** Substance use patterns

Only scored when substance use is mentioned in a session. No judgment — just pattern
tracking. Substance use often functions as a coping strategy, and noticing the pattern
is the first step.

## Flourishing Scale
**Tracks:** Overall psychological wellbeing (purpose, relationships, competence)

Measures what's going *right*, not just what's wrong. Covers whether you feel
purposeful, connected, competent, and engaged with your life.

## SWLS
**Tracks:** Life satisfaction (how you evaluate your life as a whole)

A subjective big-picture check — how close is your life to your ideal? Complements
the Flourishing Scale: flourishing tracks psychological resources, SWLS tracks your
overall evaluation.

## SCS-SF
**Tracks:** Self-compassion (kindness vs. criticism toward yourself)

How you treat yourself when things are hard predicts recovery better than almost
anything. This tracks the balance between self-kindness and self-judgment, common
humanity and isolation, mindfulness and over-identification.

## ECR-R
**Tracks:** Attachment patterns (comfort with closeness, fear of abandonment)

Tracks how relational patterns show up in your reflections — comfort with intimacy,
anxiety about rejection, tendency toward self-reliance. These patterns are deeply
rooted but can shift over time.

## MLQ
**Tracks:** Meaning in life (do you have it? are you searching for it?)

Both presence and search matter — and they change over time. Having meaning is
protective. Searching for meaning is healthy. Low on both is worth paying attention to.

## CD-RISC-10
**Tracks:** Resilience (ability to bounce back, adapt, cope)

A broad measure of psychological sturdiness — your ability to adapt, recover from
setbacks, and stay focused under pressure. Supports everything else.

## Schema Domains
**Tracks:** Deep emotional patterns (unmet needs from childhood/adolescence)

Tracks activation of core belief patterns — not a validated psychometric score, but
useful for noticing recurring themes like fear of abandonment, self-sacrifice, or
unrelenting standards.

---

## How These Impressions Work

- **Impression** is a rough, qualitative read — minimal, mild, moderate, and so on —
  loosely mapped from the scale's published cutoffs. It's an impression, not a precise
  score or a clinical rating.
- **Range** is the scale's possible minimum and maximum, shown only for context — your
  impression isn't a measured number sitting at an exact point inside it.
- **Trend** compares to your prior sessions: ↑ improving, → about the same, ↓ worsening,
  ⚠ sustained elevation worth attention.
- Not every scale is reflected every session — only the ones the conversation gives
  enough to go on.
- These reads are conservative: when something's ambiguous, the gentler interpretation
  is used.

## Important

These are inferred impressions, not tests or diagnoses. A heavier reading doesn't mean
something is wrong with you — it means a pattern is worth paying attention to. If a
pattern stays elevated over several sessions, consider talking to a therapist or
counselor. That's not failure — it's using the data well.
